The storage capacity available on the Colorado River system has allowed us to meet water demands during this sustained, 15 year drought. However, water use during the drought has exceeded supplies into the system, drawing down the stored reserves.

When water use is greater than supply, water storage is decreased as can be seen in reduced levels in reservoirs in the Upper (link to UC Basin Teacup Diagram) and Lower Colorado River basin (link to LC Basin Teacup Diagram).

Water supply and consumptive use in the Colorado River Basin

Trends in water supply and water used from the Colorado River. When water use exceeds the water supplied, water reserves are reduced. Over the past 30 years, average water supply and average water use have been about the same- under these conditions, reservoirs are much slower to be replenished. Water supply and use data above are estimated by the Bureau of Reclamation using water accounting information, USGS streamgage data, and other sources.
