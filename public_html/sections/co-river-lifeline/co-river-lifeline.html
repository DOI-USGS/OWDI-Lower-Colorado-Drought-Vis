Water from the Colorado River is essential for life in the southwest United States and Mexico. The river system:

  • Supplies more than 1 in 10 Americans water for municipal use, including drinking water.
  • Serves at least 22 federally recognized tribes.
  • Supports 4,200 megawatts of electrical generating capacity, enough to power hundreds of contractors and millions of people in eight western states.
  • Provides irrigation for more than 5.5 million acres (~8,590 square miles) of land
  • Serves 7 National Wildlife Refuges, 4 National Recreation Areas, and 11 National Parks, and supports more than $1 billion in visitor spending associated with national parks.
  • Provides habitat for threatened, endangered, and endemic species. Endemic species exist only in limited geographic locations.
