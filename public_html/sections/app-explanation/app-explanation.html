Since 2000, the Colorado River Basin has been experiencing extended drought conditions that have impacted regional water supply. The Basin is an interconnected system of rivers, reservoirs, and diversions that provides water for uses such as drinking water, agriculture, recreation, hydropower, and ecosystems. Water in the Basin is governed by a complex set of policies that allocates water among five states and two countries.

In response to the drought, water managers are working together throughout the Basin to find creative ways to lessen the impacts of the drought for the people and systems that rely on water from the Colorado River.
