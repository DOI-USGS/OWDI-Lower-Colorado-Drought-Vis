Projected water elevation for Lakes Mead and Powell determine when water shortages are severe enough to reduce water delivery to some states. The elevations are naturally dynamic through time, but are decreasing as a result of the current drought and steady use.

Projections made by the Bureau of Reclamation take into account many of the factors that affect water levels, including upper Colorado River Basin snowpack, precipitation, and projected use.
