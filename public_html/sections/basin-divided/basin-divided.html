The Colorado River Basin is divided into Upper and Lower Basins. The Upper Basin spans the states of Wyoming, Colorado, New Mexico, and Utah. The Lower Basin spans Nevada, Arizona, and California. The river also supplies water to the northern portion of the country of Mexico.

The Lower Basin is much drier than the Upper Basin and depends on deliveries from the Upper Basin for most of its Colorado River water. Of water supply from the Upper Basin, about 50% of streamflow comes from groundwater. Precipitation, primarily in the form of snow, falls on the Rocky Mountains, melts, and replenishes Colorado River Basin reservoirs.
